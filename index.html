How we choose our picks

We don’t rely on our own lab tests or quick trials. Instead, we pore over long‑term owner feedback from backpacking forums, published reviews from trusted outdoor sites, and agency approval lists (IGBC, Sierra Interagency Black Bear Group, etc.). We track what actually breaks: cracked lids after years of use, tricky closures that fail in cold weather, and models that rangers have started rejecting at permit check‑ins. Any canister we recommend must have at least one full season of consistent reports from real owners, not just launch‑week hype. We weigh capacity versus weight versus price, but we also look at less obvious factors: how the canister fits inside common packs, ease of opening with cold hands, and whether replacement parts are easy to find.
